POV: He is Legend!

August 26, 1955
Pather Panchali
The film that started it all. A lyrical, humanist masterpiece about childhood in rural Bengal. It won the "Best Human Document" award at Cannes and placed Indian cinema on the global map.
IMDb
April 17, 1964
Charulata (The Lonely Wife)
Ray's personal favorite. Set in 19th-century Bengal, this is a flawless cinematic study of loneliness, intellect, and forbidden love. It earned Ray the Silver Bear for Best Director at Berlin.
IMDb
May 8, 1969
Goopy Gyne Bagha Byne
A timeless fantasy-musical that satirizes war and tyranny. The story of two bumbling musicians granted boons by the King of Ghosts remains a cult classic for children and adults alike.
IMDb
October 10, 1958
Jalsaghar (The Music Room)
A haunting visual poem about a crumbling aristocrat who sacrifices his remaining fortune to uphold his prestige through music. A stunning reflection on the clash between tradition and modernity.
IMDb
May 6, 1966
Nayak (The Hero)
Taking place entirely on a train journey, this psychological drama deconstructs the facade of a matinee idol. It is a masterful critique of fame, insecurity, and human connection.
IMDb
October 3, 1977
Shatranj Ke Khilari
Ray's only Hindi feature film. A brilliant historical satire contrasting the obsession of two chess players with the British annexation of the Kingdom of Oudh in 1856.
